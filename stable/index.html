Definitions

How to use
  • Brief glossary for stable chest pain pathways.
  • Local protocols and radiology/cardiology workflows vary.

Core terms

  • Stable chest pain: Symptoms without features suggesting ACS/unstable ischemia.
  • Known CAD: Prior MI, prior PCI/CABG, or documented CAD on CCTA/ICA.
  • Obstructive CAD: Typically ≥50% stenosis (or flow-limiting disease by physiology).
  • Nonobstructive CAD: Plaque present but <50% stenosis.
  • GDMT: Guideline-directed medical therapy (risk-factor and symptom-focused therapy).
  • SIHD: Stable ischemic heart disease (chronic coronary syndrome framework).

Tests and modalities

  • CCTA: Coronary CT angiography (anatomic coronary imaging using iodinated contrast).
  • CAC: Coronary artery calcium score (noncontrast CT plaque burden marker).
  • ICA: Invasive coronary angiography (catheter-based coronary imaging).
  • FFR: Fractional flow reserve (invasive physiology to assess flow-limiting stenosis).
  • iFR: Instantaneous wave-free ratio (resting invasive physiology alternative to FFR).
  • FFR-CT: CT-derived estimate of FFR from CCTA data (when available).
  • Stress imaging: Stress echo, PET/SPECT MPI, or stress CMR to assess ischemia.
  • PET: Positron emission tomography (can quantify perfusion; may assess flow reserve).
  • SPECT: Single photon emission CT myocardial perfusion imaging.
  • CMR: Cardiac MRI (stress perfusion and tissue characterization).
  • MPI: Myocardial perfusion imaging (PET or SPECT perfusion assessment).

INOCA terms

  • INOCA: Ischemia with nonobstructive coronary arteries (symptoms/ischemia without obstructive CAD).
  • CMD: Coronary microvascular dysfunction (microvascular impaired vasodilation/flow).
  • VSA: Vasospastic angina (epicardial coronary spasm physiology).
  • MBFR: Myocardial blood flow reserve (PET/CMR concept; site-specific thresholds).
  • CFVR: Coronary flow velocity reserve (echo Doppler concept; site-specific thresholds).

Evidence shorthand

  • COR: Class of recommendation (strength of recommendation).
  • LOE: Level of evidence (quality/quantity of supporting evidence).
