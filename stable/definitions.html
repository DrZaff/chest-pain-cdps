Definitions

Brief glossary used across the stable chest pain pathways.

Core terms

  • Stable chest pain: Symptoms without features suggesting ACS/unstable ischemia.
  • Known CAD: Prior MI, prior PCI/CABG, or documented CAD on CCTA/ICA.
  • Obstructive CAD: Typically ≥50% stenosis or flow-limiting disease by physiology.
  • Nonobstructive CAD: Plaque present but <50% stenosis.
  • GDMT: Guideline-directed medical therapy (risk-factor and symptom-focused therapy).
  • SIHD: Stable ischemic heart disease (chronic coronary syndrome framework).

Tests and modalities

  • CCTA: Coronary CT angiography (anatomic imaging using iodinated contrast).
  • CAC: Coronary artery calcium score (noncontrast CT plaque burden marker).
  • ICA: Invasive coronary angiography (catheter-based coronary imaging).
  • FFR: Fractional flow reserve (invasive physiology for flow-limiting stenosis).
  • iFR: Instantaneous wave-free ratio (resting invasive physiology alternative).
  • FFR-CT: CT-derived estimate of FFR from CCTA data (when available).
  • PET: Positron emission tomography (may quantify perfusion/flow reserve).
  • SPECT: Single photon emission CT myocardial perfusion imaging.
  • CMR: Cardiac MRI (stress perfusion and tissue characterization).
  • MPI: Myocardial perfusion imaging (PET or SPECT).

INOCA terms

  • INOCA: Ischemia with nonobstructive coronary arteries.
  • CMD: Coronary microvascular dysfunction.
  • VSA: Vasospastic angina.
  • MBFR: Myocardial blood flow reserve (site-specific thresholds).
  • CFVR: Coronary flow velocity reserve (site-specific thresholds).

Evidence shorthand

  • COR: Class of recommendation.
  • LOE: Level of evidence.
